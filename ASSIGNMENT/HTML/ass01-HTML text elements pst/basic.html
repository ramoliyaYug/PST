Subscript and Superscript Examples

The chemical formula for glucose is C6H12O6.

The Pythagorean theorem states that a2 + b2 = c2, where a and b are the legs of a right triangle and c is the hypotenuse.

The formation of water can be represented by the equation 2H2 + O2 → 2H2O.

The speed of light in a vacuum is approximately 3 × 108 m/s.

The molecular weight of water (H2O) is 18 g/mol.

The formula for sodium chloride is NaCl, where Na is sodium and Cl is chlorine.

In scientific terms, the speed of light is approximately 3 × 108 m/s.

The gravitational force can be described by the equation F = G(m1m2)/r2.

John Dalton proposed that elements consist of atoms of varying weights. For instance, the atomic mass of Carbon is C12, which refers to the most abundant isotope.

In everyday life, we often see mathematical expressions such as E=mc2 which represents Einstein's theory of relativity.

Another common example is the formula for sulfuric acid, H2SO4, used in batteries.
